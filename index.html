2021

Not to state the obvious, but 2021 was a crazy year. Protests and riots were a huge part of first half of the year. Many people protesting agains the Covid-19 lock downs. The headlies in the news always had something about the current state of protest and riots. As the year progressed, things seemed to settle down. The Olympics occuered this summer. Many people rooting for their country. As the Olympics started up so did other sports. As the Covid-19 vaccines continue to roll-out we should be getting back to normal very soon. The light at the end of the tunnel is becoming bigger!

Timeline of 2021

January

January 4th, UK Begins Distributing AstraZeneca/Oxford Vaccine

January 5th, 2021 World Junior Ice Hockey Championship ends, in Edmonton Alberta

January 6th, mob of Donald Trump supporters stormed the united states capitol in Washington, D.C.

January 8th, Trump banned for mulitple social media platforms.

Febuary

Febuary 2nd, Jeff Bezos steps down from being CEO of Amazon after 30 years.

Febuary 19th, Pfizer/BioNTech First Dose Effective in Real-World Data

Febuary 21th, Pfizer Vaccine 98.8% Effective Against Deaths, Hospitalizations After 2 Doses

Febuary 23rd, Tiger Woods gets in car accident injurig both legs.

March

March 2nd, Dolly Parton recieves Moderna Vaccine, donates $1 Million to continue devlopment.

March 3rd,Great apes at San Diego Zoo given experimental Covid-19 vaccine for animals after outbreak amoung gorillias ealier that week.

March 14th, 63rd Grammy awards presented. Beyonce breakes record for most Grammys won by a singer(28)!

April

April 4th, 39th NCAA Women's Basketball Championship: Stanford Cardinal defeat Arizona Wildcats, 54–53.

April 5th, India records over 100,000 new COVID cases for the first time, more than half in the state of Maharashtra, which begins a new lockdown.

April 10th, China orders Alibaba pay a record fine of 18.2 billion yuan ($2.8 billion) after anti-trust regulations say it has been acting as a monopoly.

April 20th, Former police officer Derek Chauvin convicted of the murder of George Floyd in Minneapolis. Sparks nation wide protests.

May

May 1st, India is the first country to record more than 400,000 new COVID-19 cases in one day.

May 22nd, Mount Nyiragongo erupts in the Democratic Republic of Congo, sending rivers of lava towards the nearby city of Goma, prompting evacuations.

May 24th, India's official COVID-19 death toll passes 300,000 (303,720), the third country to do so, with experts saying it is a vast undercount.

May 26th, Russia reports it has started vaccinating domestic animals against COVID-19, using the Karnivak-Kov vaccine.

May 27th, "Friends" reunion screens, 17 years after the TV sitcom series ended.

June

June 7th, Two passenger trains collide in Pakistan, killing at least 40 people in Sindh province.

June 11th, US lobsterman survives being swallowed by a humpback whale of coast of Provincetown, Massachusetts.

June 24th, Second discovery of the remains of 761 people, mainly indigenous children announced at former Marieval Indian Residential School in Saskatchewan.

June 25th, Former US police officer Derek Chauvin sentenced to 22 years and six months for the murder of George Floyd in Minneapolis.

July

July 4th, Pope Fran.cis undergoes surgery to remove part of his colon in Rome.

July 8th, Japan says the Olympics will be held without spectators as Tokyo announces state of emergency due to a surge in COVID-19

July 9th, British road cyclist Mark Cavendish wins Nîmes to Carcassonne stage 13 of the Tour de France for his record equalling 34th career stage victory; equals Eddy Merckx's mark.

July 13th, 337 die in violent protests and looting in South Africa following the jailing of Jacob Zuma, with more than 200 shopping malls set alight

July 20th, Amazon founder Jeff Bezos goes into space in first unpiloted suborbital flight with all-civilian crew, aboard rocket developed by his company Blue Origin

August

August 3rd, Hawaii's largest wildfire on record, Mana Road Fire in South Kohala, finally being brought under control after burning through 40,000 acres

August 12th, US FDA authorises a third booster COVID-19 vaccine booster shot for people with weak immune systems.

August 15th, Canadian Prime Minister Justin Trudeau calls a snap election for 20 September, two years ahead of schedule, after promising not to call an early election.

August 31st, 36-year old Cristiano Ronaldo is confirmed as a Manchester United player for a second time when his transfer from Juventus is ratified; Ronaldo left United in 2009 to join Real Madrid.

September

September 1st, Cristiano Ronaldo breaks world record for goals scored in men's international football; hits his 110th and 111th goals for Portugal in 2-1 World Cup qualifying win over Republic of Ireland in Faro.

September 9th, Tom Brady becomes first player in NFL history to start 300 regular season games as he guides Tampa Bay Buccaneers to an opening day 31-29 win at home to Dallas Cowboys.

September 14th, 1 in 500 Americans now died of COVID-19 as the nations's known deathtoll reaches 663,913.

September 21st, McDonald's announces plans to "drastically" reduce plastic in its Happy Meals by 2025 (these meals make it one of largest toy distributors in the world).

October

October 1st, US COVID-19 death toll passes 700,000, with daily deaths averaging 1,900 a day.

October 5th, Nobel Prize for Physics awarded to Syukuro Manabe, Klaus Hasselmann and Giorgio Parisi for pioneering work that warned of climate change.

October 8th, Nobel Peace Prize awarded to journalists Maria Ressa of the Philippines and Dmitry Muratov of Russia "for their efforts to safeguard freedom of expression."

October 19th, Moscow's mayor orders unvaccinated people over 60 years to stay home for four months amid a worsening COVID-19 crisis.

November

Novemeber 10th, China and the US announce plans to work together on cutting greenhouse gas emissions at the COP26 summit.

November 14th, Attack on a military police outpost near a gold mine in Inata, northern Burkina Faso, kills at least 53, prompting three days of national mourning

November 18th, Longest partial lunar eclipse since 1440, lasting 3 hours, 28 minutes and 23 seconds.
